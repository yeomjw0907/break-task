# TaskBrick Strategy

## Positioning

TaskBrick is a `workday truth layer` for desk workers.

It is not primarily a task manager, a habit app, or a time tracker.
It sits on top of existing tools and answers four questions:

- When did I actually start working?
- How much of my day was real focused work?
- What did I actually finish?
- Can I close the day with a clear sense of progress?

Core product statement:

> TaskBrick helps solo operators and busy knowledge workers start the day, track real focus, manage interruptions, and close the day with a truthful work report.

## Problem

The real problem is not "I need another todo app."

The real problems are:

- People stay at their desk for a long time but do not know how much of that became real focused work.
- At the end of the day, they often cannot clearly answer "what did I actually do today?"
- Existing tools split planning, execution, time tracking, and reflection into different places.
- Completion usually has weak emotional payoff, so motivation fades.

TaskBrick should solve `workday ambiguity`, not just task storage.

## Why Now

The market already proves demand across adjacent categories:

- Daily planning and timeboxing: Sunsama
- Auto-scheduling and work planning: Motion
- Real time tracking: RescueTime, Toggl
- Gamified productivity: Todoist Karma, Habitica, LifeUp

The open space is the intersection:

- credible enough for professionals
- reflective enough to show real work patterns
- rewarding enough to feel momentum
- simple enough to live beside Notion and calendar tools

## Ideal Customer Profile

### ICP 1: Solo Operators

- freelancers
- one-person business owners
- early founders
- small agency owners

Why they care:

- their time is directly tied to output and income
- they need both execution and self-accountability
- they often use Notion + Calendar + ad hoc notes today

### ICP 2: Context-Switching Knowledge Workers

- operations
- marketing
- planning
- product coordinators

Why they care:

- they rarely work in long uninterrupted blocks
- they suffer from interruption-heavy days
- they need help resuming work after context switches

### ICP 3: Focus-Challenged Users

- users with ADHD-like working patterns
- users who start many tasks but rarely maintain structure

Why they care:

- they need strong restart loops
- they benefit from visible workday closure and short reward loops

## Current Alternatives

Most users stitch together several tools:

- Notion for planning
- calendar for time visibility
- Todoist or similar for capture
- RescueTime or Toggl for tracking
- manual notes for reflection

Main weakness:

- none of these tools unify `start work -> track focus -> manage interruptions -> close the day`

## Product Thesis

TaskBrick wins if it becomes:

`the app you open when work starts, keep nearby while work happens, and use to close the day`

That means:

- planning is light
- execution is central
- reflection is unavoidable
- rewards support the loop instead of becoming the product

## Core Loop

1. `Clock in`
2. Set `top 3` and work budget for the day
3. Start a task timer when real work begins
4. Record switches, interruptions, and resumes naturally
5. Complete tasks and receive short reward feedback
6. `Clock out`
7. Review workday truth report
8. Carry forward unfinished work to tomorrow

## Jobs To Be Done

### Functional

- Help me see the difference between time present and time focused.
- Help me finish more meaningful work in the hours I already spend.
- Help me recover quickly when my day gets fragmented.
- Help me review the day without opening five tools.

### Emotional

- Help me feel that my day counted.
- Help me reduce guilt from "I was busy but did nothing."
- Help me end the day with closure instead of drift.

### Social / Identity

- Help me become the kind of person who works deliberately, not reactively.

## Product Boundaries

### Must Be

- desktop-work oriented
- calm and professional in base UI
- rewarding at completion moments
- useful even without external integrations

### Must Not Be

- fantasy RPG productivity app
- surveillance tool
- bloated project management suite
- AI auto-scheduler in the first version

## Retention Hooks

- morning clock-in
- evening clock-out
- truthful daily report
- visible weekly trend
- short reward loop on completion

Rewards should be a retention booster, not the primary reason to pay.

## Go-To-Market

Start with users who already feel the pain sharply:

- freelancers
- solo founders
- operators
- marketers
- planners

Early channels:

- X / Twitter
- Notion and productivity communities
- freelancer and builder Discord / Slack groups
- LinkedIn

Messaging should avoid "fun todo app."

Preferred message:

- `See how much of your day became real work.`
- `Close the day with proof, not guilt.`

## Validation Experiments

### Experiment 1: Messaging

Test two landing page angles:

- A: `Workday Truth Layer`
- B: `Shutdown Coach`

Success signal:

- stronger signup intent and clearer user recall of the value proposition

### Experiment 2: 7-Day Usage

Hand-onboard 20-50 users.

Measure:

- clock-in rate
- clock-out completion rate
- daily return rate
- week 1 retention

### Experiment 3: Report Pull

Measure whether users come back primarily for:

- daily report
- score/reward
- focus timer

### Experiment 4: Paid Signal

Ask active users whether they would pay for:

- weekly review
- sync
- mini widget
- advanced analytics

## Biggest Risks

- The product feels like a toy if the game layer is too loud.
- The product feels like surveillance if tracking is too heavy.
- The message gets muddled if planning, tracking, and rewards are all pitched equally.

The safest strategic choice is to lead with:

`truthful workday visibility + daily closure`

## Recommendation

TaskBrick should not expand into a broad productivity suite yet.

It should become:

`the best personal tool for starting, tracking, and closing a real desk-work day`

That is the sharpest wedge with the strongest chance of market pull.
